Everything you need to know about how DeLectured processes, understands, and structures your audio into exhaustive study materials.

Getting Started

  • Get a Groq API Key

    DeLectured runs entirely in your browser using the Groq API for blazing fast inference. Get a free API key at console.groq.com.

  • Prepare Your Audio

    Record your lecture. We support files up to 1GB — the engine resamples and compresses client-side using lamejs @ 64kbps before uploading. Supported formats: MP3, M4A, WAV, MP4, AAC, OGG, FLAC, WebM, AMR.

  • Select Language

    DeLectured natively supports mixed-language processing. If your professor mixes Hindi and English (Hinglish), select the "Hindi / Hinglish" option. There's also an "Auto-detect" mode for other languages.

  • Upload & Wait

    Processing typically takes 1–4 minutes depending on lecture length. The audio is split into 10-minute segments and transcribed in parallel (2 concurrent streams) for maximum speed.

Privacy Architecture

Your audio never touches our servers. DeLectured is a static web application. When you upload a file, it is compressed in your browser and sent directly to Groq's secure API endpoints. We have no databases, no user accounts, and no access to your API key or data.

The Expert Pipeline

01

Audio Processing

Your file is decoded in-browser using the Web Audio API, resampled to 16kHz mono, and split into 10-minute segments with 3-second overlaps. Each segment is then async-compressed to MP3 using lamejs — yielding to the UI thread to prevent freezing.

02

Parallel Whisper-Turbo Transcription

Segments are sent to whisper-large-v3-turbo via the Groq API, 2 at a time, with automatic retry logic (3 attempts per segment). This enables massive files to be processed in minutes rather than hours.

03

Stage 1 — Domain Analysis (8B)

A fast inference call using llama-3.1-8b-instant reads the first 5,000 characters to auto-detect the academic domain (e.g., Biology, Economics, CS) and subject area. This metadata shapes everything that follows.

04

Stage 2 — Expert Structuring (70B)

The full transcript is sent to llama-3.3-70b-versatile acting as a Subject Matter Expert in the detected domain. It generates: a 500+ word technical summary, 20+ concepts with deep definitions, an interactive concept graph, lecture DNA heatmap, intelligence scores, and active recall flashcards — all in a single pass.

05

Defensive Rendering

Every visual component (scores, summary, DNA heatmap, concept map, flashcards) is rendered inside its own try-catch block. If one component fails, the rest still render perfectly — the "Big Reveal" never crashes.

Feature Deep-Dives

Concept Confidence

Not all topics are covered equally. DeLectured extracts each concept with a confidence rating (1–3). This tells you exactly what was deeply explained vs. briefly mentioned — so you know what to look up in the textbook.

Interactive Concept Map

A live, animated canvas visualization connects extracted concepts with labeled relationships. Nodes drift with subtle physics, pulse gently, and are rendered using the Paper & Ink palette. This gives you a bird's-eye view of how concepts relate to each other.

Lecture DNA Heatmap

A visual density heatmap across 20 segments of the lecture. Tall bars = high-density content. Short bars = lighter sections. At a glance, you see exactly where the professor front-loaded theory vs. wound down with examples.

Active Recall Flashcards

Auto-generated question/answer cards from the lecture content. Click to flip. Designed for active recall study sessions — the most effective memorization technique backed by cognitive science.

Annotations & Q&A

After processing, you can ask follow-up questions directly in the annotations panel. DeLectured uses llama-3.1-8b-instant with streaming responses, grounded in your lecture notes, to give instant academic insights — like a TA that actually read the material.

Intelligence Scores

Five metrics scored by the 70B model: Clarity (how well-explained), Content Density (information per minute), Pace (speaking speed appropriateness), Concept Count (total concepts found), and Revision Time (estimated study minutes).

FAQ

What if my recording is larger than 25MB?

No problem. DeLectured handles files up to 1GB. Your audio is decoded, resampled to 16kHz mono, and compressed to 64kbps MP3 entirely in your browser before being sent to the API in 10-minute segments.

Why do I need my own API key?

Because DeLectured has no backend and is completely free. Providing your own Groq key allows you to use their extremely fast inference models at no cost to you (within their generous free tier).

Can I save my notes?

Yes. Use the "Download Full Report" button to get a .TXT file containing the summary, concepts, and full transcript. You can also use "Print PDF" for a formatted version. Because there is no database, refreshing the page will clear your current notes.

What models does DeLectured use?

Transcription: whisper-large-v3-turbo via Groq. Domain Analysis: llama-3.1-8b-instant for fast classification. Study Guide Generation: llama-3.3-70b-versatile for exhaustive structuring. Q&A Chat: llama-3.1-8b-instant with streaming.

Can I process another lecture after finishing?

Yes. After results are displayed, use the "Process Another Lecture" button at the bottom to reset and start fresh.

What's the concept map?

An interactive, animated canvas visualization where extracted concepts are shown as nodes connected by labeled relationships. The nodes gently drift and pulse — it's both useful and mesmerizing.
